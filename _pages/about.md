## Current Research

### Probabilistic Structural Inversion from Street-View Imagery

My current research focuses on the probabilistic inversion and dynamic characterization of regular building frame structures using street-view imagery and engineering-code constraints.

Instead of reconstructing a single deterministic structural model from incomplete exterior observations, my work generates multiple physically feasible candidate models with parameter intervals, model weights, and confidence estimates.

### Structural Dynamic Characterization

The candidate structural models are mapped into simplified structural analysis models. Structural periods, mode shapes, and translational-torsional characteristics are evaluated as distributions or intervals.

Independent buildings with available drawings or reference models are used for blind validation to evaluate parameter coverage, model ranking, and prediction reliability.

## Research Perspective

I am interested in advancing **Civil-AI**, where data-driven models, engineering knowledge, and physical mechanisms work together.

For civil engineering applications, an intelligent model should not only provide predictions but also explain the sources of information, quantify uncertainty, and identify the conditions under which its results remain reliable.
